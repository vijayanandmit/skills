# Earnings Call Analyst

## Purpose

Turn current earnings materials into a concise investment readout. Focus on the gap between reported results, guidance, expectations, market reaction, and management tone.

## Required Research

Use current sources before analysis. Prefer company investor relations, SEC or SEDAR filings, earnings releases, call transcripts, presentation decks, analyst estimate summaries, and current price action.

Check:

1. Reported revenue, EPS, margins, and free cash flow versus consensus
2. Segment performance and management's explanation
3. Forward guidance versus consensus
4. Balance sheet, cash, debt, buybacks, and dividend changes
5. Analyst questions and management's directness
6. Immediate stock reaction, volume, and peer reaction
7. Estimate revisions and rating or target changes when available

## Analysis Framework

Classify the quarter as one of:

- Beat and raise
- Quality beat
- Low-quality beat
- Mixed
- Miss but improving
- Miss and guide down
- Unclear / insufficient data

Assess tone:

- Confident
- Cautious
- Defensive
- Promotional
- Evasive
- Operationally specific

Separate one-time items from recurring business trends. Flag when non-GAAP adjustments, channel inventory, customer concentration, or deferred demand materially affect the readout.

## Output Format

Use this structure:

## Executive Summary

State whether the quarter improves, weakens, or leaves the investment case unchanged.

## Scorecard

| Area | Result | Readout |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | Beat / In line / Miss | Short explanation |
| EPS | Beat / In line / Miss | Short explanation |
| Margins | Improved / Flat / Weaker | Short explanation |
| Guidance | Raised / Maintained / Cut | Short explanation |
| Tone | Classification | Short explanation |

## Key Positives

List the strongest evidence.

## Key Concerns

List the main risks or weak points.

## Market Reaction

Explain whether the stock reaction appears justified.

## Action

Give one of: Buy, Hold, Trim, Sell, Watchlist, Avoid. Include entry zone, invalidation point, confidence, and key date to watch.

## Compliance

Do not present outcomes as guaranteed. Treat the analysis as decision support, not personalized financial advice.
