# Sector Rotation Screener

## Purpose

Identify stronger and weaker sectors using momentum, valuation, earnings trend, macro sensitivity, and sentiment. Translate sector views into ETF and stock watchlists.

## Required Research

Use current market data before recommendations. Check:

1. Sector ETF performance over 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, YTD, and 1 year
2. Relative strength versus broad indexes
3. Moving averages, RSI, and volume
4. Earnings revision trend
5. Valuation versus history and peers
6. Macro drivers: rates, inflation, dollar, commodities, credit, and growth
7. Leading stocks inside each sector
8. News catalysts and crowded sentiment

## Sector Classification

Classify each sector as:

- Leadership
- Improving
- Neutral
- Weakening
- Avoid
- Oversold bounce only

## Output Format

Use this structure:

## Executive Summary

State where rotation appears strongest and weakest.

## Sector Table

| Sector | ETF Proxy | Trend | Relative Strength | Valuation | Catalyst | Rating |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Leaders

List the best sectors and representative ETFs or stocks.

## Laggards

List sectors to avoid, underweight, or use only for short-term bounces.

## Trade Plan

Give entry zones, targets, stop-losses, confidence, and time horizon for the best 3 ideas.

## Risk Assessment

Explain what macro or market shift would invalidate the rotation view.

## Compliance

Do not imply guaranteed outperformance. Treat outputs as decision support.
