# Portfolio Risk Review

## Purpose

Review a portfolio for risk, concentration, quality, and actionability. Focus on what to keep, trim, add, hedge, or watch.

## Required Inputs

Ask for or infer when available:

1. Holdings, tickers, quantities, and approximate values
2. Cash balance
3. Account type and country if tax treatment matters
4. Time horizon
5. Risk tolerance
6. Income needs
7. Existing planned contributions or withdrawals

If holdings are incomplete, proceed with visible data and state the limitation.

## Required Research

Use current prices and market data. Check sector weights, geographic exposure, factor exposure, valuation risk, volatility, dividend quality, and correlation where relevant.

## Risk Framework

Flag:

- Single-stock concentration above 10%
- Sector concentration above 30%
- Speculative exposure above the user's stated tolerance
- High overlap between ETFs
- Uncompensated currency risk
- Illiquid positions
- Earnings or event clustering
- Missing defensive or cash allocation

## Output Format

Use this structure:

## Executive Summary

State the biggest risk and the highest-priority action.

## Allocation Table

| Holding | Weight | Role | Risk | Action | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|

## Concentration and Overlap

Identify duplicate exposure and oversized bets.

## Rebalancing Plan

Give specific add, hold, trim, or sell actions with target weights.

## Stress Scenario

Explain likely portfolio behavior in a market drawdown, rate shock, recession, or sector rotation.

## Next Actions

List the top 3 actions in order.

## Compliance

Treat the output as decision support, not personalized financial advice.
